# Meeting Prep Output Patterns

Use these output shapes to keep the brief short, factual, and commercially useful.

## Single-meeting brief

```markdown
## Meeting
- [date/time]
- [account / opportunity / partner]
- [meeting type]

## Objective
- Verified: [why the meeting exists]
- Based on current opportunity stage, this suggests: [the commercial objective]

## Account snapshot
- Verified: [stage / status / next action]
- Verified: [one key company fact]
- Verified: [one relevant recent development]

## External attendees
- [name] — Verified: [title if confirmed]
- [name] — Verified: [title if confirmed]
- Gap: [identity/title uncertainty]

## What history says
- Verified: [last material interaction]
- Verified: [open commitment / blocker]
- Based on prior interactions, this suggests: [watchout]

## Likely objections or tensions
- Verified: [if explicit in history]
- Based on prior interactions, this suggests: [likely friction]

## Recommended strategy
- Lead with: [angle]
- Confirm: [decision / timeline / blocker]
- Avoid: [topic or claim to avoid]

## Assets to bring
- [asset] — why it helps
- [asset] — why it helps

## Open questions / gaps
- [missing information that still matters]
```

## Daily digest

Use one compact block per meeting in chronological order:

```markdown
### [time] [account] — [meeting title]
- Objective: [one line]
- Watchout: [one line]
- Best angle: [one line]
```

## Compression rules

- Prefer 1–3 bullets per section.
- If evidence is thin, shorten rather than invent.
- Put the highest-risk item near the top.
- Use `Verified:` and `Based on prior interactions, this suggests:` labels when mixing facts with interpretation.
