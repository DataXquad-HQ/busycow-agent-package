# Sales Asset Shapes

Choose the asset shape from audience + purpose, not from aesthetics.

## Executive summary
Use when:
- senior stakeholder needs the commercial point quickly
- the ask is alignment, sponsorship, or decision acceleration

Sections:
- situation
- why it matters now
- proposed approach
- expected outcome
- next step

## One-pager
Use when:
- a champion needs something easy to forward
- the asset must stand alone without a live presenter

Sections:
- problem
- approach
- why us
- deployment reality
- next conversation

## Partner explainer
Use when:
- external partner needs a clear positioning frame
- co-selling or referral logic matters

Sections:
- what this product is
- where it fits
- where it does not fit
- how to position it correctly
- best next action

## Workflow narrative
Use when:
- the buyer needs to imagine the operational flow
- product value is clearer in sequence than in bullets

Structure:
1. starting condition
2. workflow step-by-step
3. where value appears
4. what changes for the customer team

## Claim discipline
Every customer-facing claim should be one of:
- verified by product truth
- verified by user-provided context
- explicitly framed as a possible fit or recommendation
