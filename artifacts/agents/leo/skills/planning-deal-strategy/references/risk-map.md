# Deal Strategy Risk Map

Use this rubric to decide which risks belong in the strategy pack.

## Keep only material risks

A risk belongs in the pack if it could reasonably:
- delay the opportunity this period
- reduce close probability meaningfully
- force a different stakeholder strategy
- require a different commercial or delivery path

## Risk categories

### 1. Timing credibility
Questions:
- Is there a real decision date or procurement milestone?
- Is the close date evidenced or just optimistic?
- What must happen before the next stage is real?

### 2. Stakeholder coverage
Questions:
- Do we have the workflow owner?
- Do we have the economic or executive sponsor?
- Is an implementation or technical owner still missing?

### 3. Commercial / procurement blockers
Questions:
- Is budget confirmed, assumed, or absent?
- Are procurement, security, legal, or partner terms visible?
- Is pricing shape already a likely obstacle?

### 4. Product-fit or deployment risk
Questions:
- Has the account validated the deployment model they need?
- Are they asking for something outside current product truth?
- Is the use case still too vague to support a proposal?

### 5. Partner / dependency risk
Questions:
- Does this opportunity depend on a third party, reseller, or hardware vendor?
- Is that dependency active, stalled, or unconfirmed?

## Recommendation posture

- `push now` — evidence is good enough to advance confidently
- `tighten` — promising, but needs one missing proof point
- `multi-thread` — stakeholder gap is the main risk
- `de-risk commercially` — procurement/budget issue dominates
- `downgrade confidence` — stage or timing is ahead of evidence
