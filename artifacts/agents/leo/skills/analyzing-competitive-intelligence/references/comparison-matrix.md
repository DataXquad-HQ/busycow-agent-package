# Competitive Comparison Matrix

Build the matrix only on dimensions that matter to the live account.

## Recommended columns
- Dimension
- Us
- Competitor
- Why it matters for this account
- Evidence quality

## Good comparison dimensions for business-line opportunities
- offline / disconnected-environment capability
- deployment control and data sovereignty
- workflow fit for field teams
- implementation complexity
- reliance on cloud connectivity
- procurement or partner-model implications

## Evidence-quality labels
- `verified` — explicit source-backed fact
- `strong inference` — high-confidence interpretation from multiple facts
- `weak inference` — mention only if commercially useful and clearly labelled

## Red-line rules
- Do not claim competitor pricing unless explicitly sourced.
- Do not claim competitor customer logos unless verified.
- Do not imply product gaps we cannot evidence.
- Do not turn category assumptions into competitor facts.

## Objection-handling pattern
For each likely objection:
1. What they may say
2. What is actually true
3. Our strongest honest counter
4. When not to push that counter
