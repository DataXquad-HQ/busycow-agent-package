# Runbook — Decision Recording

Purpose: convert discussion into a decision candidate or approved decision record without overstating approval.

Use when:
- a meeting, chat, or handoff seems to contain a real decision
- policy, pricing, strategy, authority, or cross-agent behavior may have changed

Execution pattern:
1. State the proposed decision in one sentence.
2. Separate verified facts, interpretation, and open questions.
3. Check for owner, scope, rationale, approval status, and downstream task implications.
4. If approval is unclear, treat it as a draft decision candidate.
5. Route policy / authority / cross-agent implications into review instead of publishing directly.

Default companion skills:
- `review-queue-triage` when approval or governance is unresolved
- `task-routing` when the decision creates execution work
